Redundant inlines

These inlines you're removing aren't redundant, they're needed to give the variable templates external linkage, without them we can't depend on their address being the same amongst different TUs (demo) --Ybab321 (talk) 07:21, 24 July 2024 (PDT)

They are. See CWG issue 2387, which is just not implemented by GCC yet. Opened GCC Bugzilla #116077. --Fruderica (talk) 10:36, 24 July 2024 (PDT)
Ah, very good! I saw that wording in the standard and disregarded it thinking that "non-template variables" refer to templated variables such as static data members of class templates and did not refer to variable templates (and of course the note doesn't clarify anything because of the existence of inline). I'm quite happy with this result, thanks for proving me wrong :D --Ybab321 (talk) 11:56, 24 July 2024 (PDT)
